Simple present tense and Present continuous tense


Simple present tense: The simple present tense is used to describe habits, general truths, and regular actions. The simple present tense is like talking about your daily routine.

Rule: Subject + Verb in the base form/third person plural form + the rest of the sentence.

For He/She/It (add 's' or 'es' to the verb):

  • He runs fast.
  • She eats an apple.
  • It rains a lot.

For I/You/We/They (use the base form of the verb):

  • I like to read.
  • You play with the ball.
  • We sing a song.
  • They jump high.

Present continuous tense: We use this tense to talk about actions happening right now or around this time.

Rule: Subject + am/is/are + present participle (verb+ing) + the rest of the sentence
We use:

  • "is" for he/she/it
  • "am" for I
  • "are" for you/we/they

Using "is" for he/she/it:

  • He is running fast.
  • She is eating an apple.
  • It is raining outside.

Using "am" I:

  • I am reading a book.

Using "are" for you/we/they:

  • You are playing with the ball.
  • We are singing a song.
  • They are jumping high.

Keynotes:
Simple Present Tense:

  • Use it for things that happen regularly or are always true.
  • Use it for facts (e.g., The Earth orbits the Sun).

Present Continuous Tense:

  • Use it for things happening right now or around this time.
  • It's like catching someone in the act!
